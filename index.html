Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

The Missile man of India

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, The former President of India.
Born - 15th October 1931
Death - 27th July 2015
  • Dr. Kalam, is considered as one of the most distinguished Scientists of India, with unique honor of receiving doctorates from 48 Universities and Institutions from India and abroad.
  • He contributed to the first indigenously launched satellite launch vehicle (SLV-3) programme as Project Director, which successfully injected ROHINI satellite in the near earth orbit in 1980.
  • HIs leadership will be always remembered for a programme he initiated at Indian Space Research Organizations for PSLV configuration.
  • Took up the responsibility of designing and developing indigenous Guided Missiles at Defence Research and Development Organization as the Chief Executive of the Programme.
  • Spearheaded the development and operationalization of AGNI and PRITHVI Missile systems indigenously by networking of multiple institutions.
  • He was instrumental in successfully testing India's Nuclear warheads in 1998 at Pokran Range.
  • Propagated the concept to make India self-reliant in defence systems development by progressing multiple projects and development tasks.
  • Initiated a scheme to uplift rural lifestyle by initiating a programme named PURA ( Providing Urban Amenities to Rural Areas) by leveraging science and technology.
  • An accomplished writer, who authored number of books, such as: "Wings of Fire", "India 2020- A Vision for the New Millennium" and "Ignited Minds-Unleashing the power within India." among many.
  • In 1981 he was awarded with the coveted civilian award Padma Bhushan and Padma Vibhushan in 1990. He was also awarded with hightest civilian award Bharat Ratna in 1997.
  • He served as the 11th President of India.
